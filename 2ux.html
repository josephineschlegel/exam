Basic User Expereince

Data Collection

The purpose of the Data Collection assignment was to gain a clear understanding of the different methods one may collect data in future circumstances, which proved to be very important later in the course. Feedback from others really helped me throughout the first semester. Some of the data collection methods that we practiced during this theme I continue to use, for example interviews. The interview gave me an intermate understanding of how the subject thinks and feels towards being interviewed. The fact that I conducted this with one of my good friends gave a vastly different result regarding how the question were answered. When trying to interview someone I didn’t know as well later on the course, I observed there were vast differences in not only their body language, but also answers. It appeared that my friends were more honest with their responses rather than the acquittances are, which has provided something to think about when choosing who to interview next time.

We also practiced desk research, observational research and surveys. I found that I gained the least knowledge from observational research, perhaps because I didn’t get a concrete data, yet due to this exercise I understand the importance of observational research in some circumstances, for example I found that the general public would look at their phones if they were more relaxed, but if stressed for time they would be looking for the bus. Desk research and surveys also proved very useful for gathering information of what the general public want and believe.

In this assignment we were asked to present our findings and analyse the information. Whilst creating the document I was under the impression that each method had to be on one page each, whilst trying to add analysis on each page, resulting in a document filled with too much text and not enough analysis. For future assignments, I will create a separate page where I thoroughly analysis the information in question.

Content Production

The purpose of this assignment was to learn the basics of copywriting. Our assignment in hand was to copywrite a recipe from my home country. I chose to copy write a recipe of Yorkshire puddings and used what I had previous learnt through data collection to conduct an interview with one of my class mates to figure out his eating habits, in order to know what I should focus the recipe on (for example, whether I should focus on time or how much food costs).

During this assignment we were also introduced to card soring, site maps and concept maps. Whilst reviewing this document I see that my understanding of the importance of a concept map has developed much throughout the first semester.

Low Fidelity Prototype

In order to get some feedback on our idea regarding how to create a functional student housing website, we created a low fidelity prototype which included the main features we thought necessary to include. After creating this we tested it on some other classmates and recorded the process. During this we learnt that there was not a need for as many different pages, along with some major flaws, for example not being able to return to the home page from the inbox. This shaped how we would change our High-Fidelity Prototype.

Design Sprint Document

In the basic UX theme, we learnt many different methods of obtaining user opinions and expectations, also how to plan design aspects and conceptualize ideas. For the design sprint our group were dedicated to trying to do many of those as possible. Firstly, we started with the mapping and team canvas, whilst giving individuals specific roles to avoid any disagreement. Along with discussing the possible negative outcomes, we knew that the biggest challenge for this assignment was to meet the induvial deadlines of each day as we didn’t have long to complete the task in hand, therefore we took advantage to having many group members and divided the tasks equally so each one of us played a small part. This proved to help in working dynamic a lot and although there were some issues throughout, it was a significant change than the last time, I believe this was due to team roles.

To get an understanding of what students at KEA wanted, we conducted interviews with some students in the semesters above who are involved with student activities. After discussing that we strived to all agree on what idea we were going to go with. We did this by using different group methods, including lightning demos, the four-step sketch and action steps and votes. This proved to be a really good method to use as everyone had a say in what idea he or she liked the most, as well as giving light to everyone’s ideas. The idea with the most supervotes in turn got chosen, where we then proceeded to create a low fidelity prototype based on our style tile.
